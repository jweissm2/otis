2024 Cultural Olympiad Poster Competition

The City of Paris and the County of Los Angeles with additional partners organized a poster competition for the Cultural Olympiad of Paris 2024 and the run-up to the 2028 Olympic and Paralympic Games in L.A.

The competition promotes cultural exchange and collaboration by bringing together students from art schools in Paris and Los Angeles to showcase creativity, cooperation, competition, Olympic themes, and civic engagement through art and design.

Participants were invited to create a poster illustrating the Olympic hand-over between Paris and Los Angeles. At the close of the competition, 24 posters will be displayed publicly in Paris and on the www.paris.fr website. They will also be displayed digitally and via social media in Los Angeles, with additional opportunities for exhibition and/or events in person and online.

Course Integration

To ensure a broad representation of designs, Otis College integrated the poster competition into its Graphic Design IV course, which requires students to experiment with form and media by building increasingly complex visual systems and narratives.
 
The course and its learning outcomes were well aligned with the goals of the competition and all students participated. Of the 28 posters created, the top 12 were selected by a committee of core Graphic Design faculty.

Our Process

Led by Otis faculty members Davey Whitcraft and Frankie Hamersma, students engaged in a comprehensive design process. This involved thorough research, brainstorming sessions, and iterative development to capture the essence of the Olympic spirit and the cultural vibrancy of Paris. Each step was meticulously crafted to ensure that the final posters were visually compelling, conceptually strong, and aligned with the competition's themes and criteria. The resulting designs not only demonstrate the students' creative skills but also their ability to communicate powerful messages through graphic design.
